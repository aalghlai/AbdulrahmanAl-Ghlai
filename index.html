About

Hi! My name is Abdulrahman Al-Ghlai, and welcome to my personal portfolio. I am a first-generation American of Libyan heritage, born in Egypt. In 2009, my family and I moved to the United States, where I grew up with my parents and now four younger brothers. I am currently pursuing a bachelor's in Statistics and Data Science at UC Berkeley, where my academic interests are focused on AI, data science, and statistical analysis.

In addition to my studies, I am passionate about both playing and watching a wide range of sports. I enjoy building and creating, whether it's through coding projects or hands-on work. My dedication to these fields reflects my love for problem-solving and continuous learning. I strive to apply my skills to real-world challenges, particularly in the areas of data, analytics, and innovative technologies!

By the way, check out my experience page and my awesome work where you'll find some of the exciting projects I've been working on, showcasing my skills in data science, AI, and more!

Experience

Data Science Intern @ The Mango Jelly

At The Mango Jelly, I am collaborating with a team to develop AI-powered tools using Llama2 and Contextual Retrieval for Multimodal RAG to automate data extraction across a wide range of data types. My work focused on enhancing insights and system efficiency by streamlining data retrieval processes. I also worked on the implementation of PyMuPDF as an alternative to LlamaParse, which significantly reduced operational costs and improved multimodal system performance. Additionally, we are integrating these tools into the development of an AI chatbot for our website, aimed at enhancing user interaction and providing personalized support. I also optimized LlamaIndex pipelines for real-time data integration, ensuring smooth analytics across platforms. Through these efforts, I improved retrieval accuracy and reduced system response times by fine-tuning models and optimizing prompts.

Software Engineer Intern @ DT Master Carbon

As a Software Engineer Intern at DT Master Carbon, I worked on both the front-end and back-end of web applications using HTML, CSS, and Python. My main focus was improving the user interface and functionality to create a better experience for users. Since the goal was to attract paying customers, I concentrated on building features that would add real value and make users more likely to upgrade to paid services. I also helped set up development environments and tested customer-facing features to ensure they were solving key problems and encouraging more people to sign up. By optimizing APIs for smoother data exchange between the client and server, I was able to boost system performance and reduce latency, making the overall experience faster and more enjoyable for users.

Office Associate @ EPSON Middle East

During my summer at EPSON Middle East in Misrata, Libya, I handled everyday office tasks like managing schedules, organizing records, and making sure things ran smoothly. I assisted with data entry and other administrative tasks, helping to keep everything accurate and up to date. I also helped coordinate meetings and kept communication flowing between departments, which made it easier to track projects and get things done on time. It was a lot of simple, day-to-day work, but it helped keep the office organized and running efficiently.

Projects

NBA Player Salary Predictor

In Fall 2024, I developed a linear regression model to predict NBA player salaries based on their performance and key stats from the previous season. Using Pandas and NumPy, I refined the model to improve accuracy and make better salary projections. I also used Matplotlib to visualize the relationships between player stats and predicted salaries, offering insights into how different aspects of performance can influence future earnings.

Housing Price Predictor

In Summer 2024, I developed a linear regression model to predict house sale prices by comparing actual prices with the predicted outcomes. I used Matplotlib and Seaborn to visualize key factors that affect housing prices, which helped me better understand trends and market influences. By analyzing features like location and square footage, I was able to provide actionable insights into how these factors impact the predicted sale prices.

Titanic Investigation Analysis

In Summer 2024, I worked on analyzing the Titanic dataset to figure out what factors influenced passenger survival. I cleaned up the data by handling missing values and created new features like age groups and family size to get a clearer picture. Using tools like Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn, I visualized survival rates based on things like gender, passenger class, and where people boarded the ship. These visualizations helped reveal some key insights into how different factors affected survival chances.
